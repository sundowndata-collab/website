Our Story

We Built Sundown Because
the Best Moment in Africa
Should Belong to You

There is a moment in the Masai Mara that changes people. The sun drops below the acacia line. The air goes still. A lion exhales twenty metres from your vehicle and the sound travels through your chest. You will remember it for the rest of your life.

We have been operating private safaris out of Nairobi for seven years. In that time we have watched the industry change in ways that worry us. Vehicles doubled. Sighting queues grew. Guides started managing group schedules instead of reading wildlife. The moment that changes people started getting crowded out.

Sundown Safaris was built around a single conviction: the most extraordinary wildlife encounter you will ever have should not be shared with seven strangers on a different schedule. It should belong entirely to you and the people you chose to bring.

Every vehicle we operate carries a maximum of four guests. Not because the regulations require it. Because anything more compromises the silence, the positioning, and the patience that turns a sighting into a memory.

The Guide Changes Everything

Our guides have tracked these corridors for years. They know individual animal territories, family compositions, and the subtle signs that tell them where to be twenty minutes before anyone else arrives. You cannot replicate that with a map and a radio report.

Land Nobody Else Can Enter

We hold active licences in Mara North and Olare Motorogi conservancies. These are private wilderness corridors bordering the Masai Mara where group tours are not permitted. Night drives. Off road access. Three vehicles maximum at any sighting. The experience is categorically different.

Accountability You Can Check

We are KATO bonded and TRA licensed. Every vehicle in our fleet is independently audited. Every guide carries a certified Kenya Wildlife Service credential. We name our guides. We describe our vehicles. We tell you exactly what is included before you commit to anything.

Peak season (July–October): 9–12 months in advance. Shoulder season: 4–6 months. With a maximum of 4 guests per vehicle, our capacity is genuinely limited. Kenya park fees increased significantly in 2026 — clients who book early lock in current pricing. The earlier you move, the more options you have.

Kenya's safari regions — Masai Mara, Amboseli, Samburu, Laikipia — are extremely safe. These are remote wilderness areas with very low population density. No elevated travel advisories from the UK FCO or US State Department. Hundreds of thousands of tourists visit safely every year. The wildlife is the only thing that should make you nervous.

July–October is peak migration with the most wildlife activity — and the most crowds. January–February is dry season, exceptional predator sightings, and far fewer tourists — our personal favourite. June offers lush green landscapes and building migration herds at lower cost. There is no bad time. Only different experiences.
