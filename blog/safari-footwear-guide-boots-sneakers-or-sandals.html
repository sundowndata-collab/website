Safari Footwear Guide: Boots, Sneakers, or Sandals?

Published: August 20, 2025  ·  Sundown Safaris Field Team

When preparing for a safari in Kenya, most travelers focus on packing binoculars, cameras, and safari jackets — but one of the most overlooked essentials is footwear. Your shoes determine not only your comfort but also your safety, especially if your itinerary includes game drives, bush walks, or cultural visits. With terrain ranging from dusty savannahs to muddy trails during the rainy season, the question becomes: What’s the best safari footwear? Boots, sneakers, or sandals?

This guide breaks down the pros, cons, and best scenarios for each, ensuring your feet are prepared for Africa’s wilderness.

Why Safari Footwear Matters More Than You Think

Unlike a city vacation, a safari exposes you to dust, thorns, uneven terrain, mud, and wildlife environments. Poor shoe choice could mean:

  • Blisters from long walks
  • Slipping on wet grass or rocks
  • Insect bites on exposed feet
  • Discomfort during full-day game drives

Your shoes are your first line of defense against nature — and the wrong pair could ruin your dream trip.

Option 1: Safari Boots 🥾

Best for: Walking safaris, rainy/muddy conditions, rugged terrain.

  • Advantages:
    • Excellent ankle support on rough ground
    • Sturdy soles protect against thorns, sharp rocks, or uneven tracks
    • Durable and long-lasting
    • Offers better protection against snake bites or insect stings
  • Disadvantages:
    • Heavy and less breathable than sneakers
    • Can get hot during midday sun
    • Takes time to break in before the trip

Pro Tip: Choose lightweight hiking boots made of breathable fabric. Avoid steel-toe or military boots as they’re too heavy. Brands like Merrell, Columbia, and Salomon are popular among seasoned safari travelers.

Option 2: Sneakers 👟

Best for: Game drives, lodge wear, short bush walks in dry conditions.

  • Advantages:
    • Lightweight and comfortable
    • Easy to pack and versatile (great for travel days, airports, and casual lodge evenings)
    • Breathable fabric reduces sweat buildup
    • Quick drying if washed or exposed to light rain
  • Disadvantages:
    • Poor grip on wet or rocky ground
    • Little to no ankle support
    • Less protection against sharp thorns or snakes

Pro Tip: Opt for neutral-colored sneakers (tan, brown, or olive). White sneakers will be red with dust by the end of day one.

Option 3: Sandals 🩴

Best for: Lodge relaxation, beach extensions (e.g., Diani or Malindi), short walks around camp.

  • Advantages:
    • Lightweight and breathable
    • Great for hot afternoons and evenings
    • Easy to slip on/off after long game drives
    • Perfect if your safari extends to the Kenyan coast
  • Disadvantages:
    • No protection during bush walks
    • Feet exposed to insects and dust
    • Slippery when wet

Pro Tip: Bring a pair of closed-toe safari sandals like Teva or Keen. Avoid flimsy flip-flops unless just for the pool.

What Do Safari Guides Wear?

Most Kenyan safari guides wear rugged boots or sneakers depending on the activity. For bush walks, boots are non-negotiable, while sneakers are acceptable for vehicle-based safaris. Sandals are reserved for downtime at camp.

The Winning Formula: Pack All Three

If space allows, the perfect safari footwear combination is:

  • 1 Pair of Lightweight Boots → for walks and rugged terrain
  • 1 Pair of Neutral Sneakers → for drives and casual evenings
  • 1 Pair of Sandals → for camp relaxation or beach extensions

This three-shoe combo ensures comfort, safety, and versatility across all safari settings.

Highlight:

The best safari footwear isn’t about choosing boots vs sneakers vs sandals — it’s about knowing when to wear which pair. Pack smart, prioritize comfort, and your safari will be as unforgettable as the wildlife you came to see.
