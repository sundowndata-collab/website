Can You Book a Last-Minute Migration Safari in Kenya?

Published: August 20, 2025  ·  Sundown Safaris Field Team

The Myth of “Too Late”

You’ve seen the photos of wildebeest river crossings, and now you want in—but it’s already July or August. The migration is on, lodges are filling up, and flights are rising. Is it too late to plan?

Good news: It’s possible—but you’ll need flexibility, quick decisions, and maybe a touch of luck. Here’s how to book a last-minute migration safari in Kenya.

1. The Challenge of Late Planning

  • High Demand: July–October in the Masai Mara = peak safari season.
  • Limited Beds: Many top camps book 6–12 months in advance.
  • Price Surge: Expect to pay 20–40% more than early-bookers.

2. What Makes It Possible

  • Cancellations: High-end lodges sometimes free up rooms last-minute.
  • Offbeat Camps: Community conservancies near Mara offer availability when central Mara camps are full.
  • Flight Flexibility: Early morning departures from Nairobi sometimes open up just days before.

3. Strategies for Booking Late

  1. Be Flexible with Dates: Weekdays have more availability than weekends.
  2. Consider Conservancies: Olare Motorogi, Naboisho, and Ol Kinyei often have space.
  3. Mix Lodging Levels: Combine luxury and mid-range camps to fit budget + availability.
  4. Use Safari Specialists: Local operators often get insider cancellation updates before platforms do.

4. Last-Minute Cost Hacks

  • Group Tours: Join scheduled departures instead of booking private safaris.
  • Drive vs Fly: Driving may be easier to arrange than booking last-minute flights.
  • Stay Longer in Fewer Camps: Avoid constant transfers—focus on Mara.

5. What to Expect

  • You WILL see the migration. The herds move in massive numbers.
  • Don’t expect Instagram-famous camps. Focus on the wildlife, not the lodge.
  • Book fast: Last-minute options disappear within hours.

Insider Tip from Guides

“Last-minute travelers often get some of the wildest sightings, because flexibility allows them to chase herd movements rather than stick to rigid bookings.”

Short Takeaway:

Yes, you can book a last-minute migration safari—but flexibility is your currency.
