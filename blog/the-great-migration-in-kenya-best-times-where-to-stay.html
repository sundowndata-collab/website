The Great Migration in Kenya: Best Times & Where to Stay

Published: July 22, 2025  ·  Sundown Safaris Field Team

Introduction: Nature's Greatest Show on Earth

Few wildlife spectacles compare to the Great Migration—a timeless journey of over 1.5 million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles moving in a loop through the Serengeti and Maasai Mara. Kenya hosts the most dramatic moments of this event: river crossings, predator ambushes, and breathtaking landscapes.

For any safari lover, experiencing the Great Migration in Kenya is a bucket-list adventure. But timing and location are everything. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you plan the perfect migration safari, from the best months to travel to where you should stay.

What Is the Great Migration?

The Great Migration is an annual, circular movement of herbivores that begins in Tanzania’s Serengeti and extends into Kenya’s Maasai Mara. The animals follow rainfall patterns in search of fresh grass, covering nearly 3,000 kilometers.

Key Participants:

  • 1.3+ million wildebeest
  • 300,000+ gazelles
  • 200,000 zebras
  • Predators like lions, cheetahs, crocodiles, and hyenas

While the migration is continuous, the most famous portion—the Mara River crossings—occurs in Kenya between July and October.

Best Time to Witness the Migration in Kenya

July to October is considered the prime migration season in Kenya, particularly in the Maasai Mara. Here's how the months break down:

  • July: First herds start arriving in the Mara. Dramatic river crossings begin.
  • August: Peak river crossing season. Most thrilling predator-prey interactions.
  • September: Herds remain in the Mara feeding and regrouping.
  • October: The final groups cross back into the Serengeti.

Tip: River crossings are unpredictable. Booking at least a 3-5 night stay increases your chances of witnessing the action.

Where to Stay During the Great Migration

1. Mara Naboisho Conservancy

  • Why here: Just northeast of the main reserve, Naboisho offers similar migration access with fewer crowds.
  • Top Lodges: Naboisho Camp, Leopard Hill
  • Unique Edge: Off-road access and walking safaris

2. Mara North Conservancy

  • Why here: Adjacent to the Mara Reserve with top-tier wildlife sightings
  • Top Lodges: Elephant Pepper Camp, Karen Blixen Camp
  • Unique Edge: Exclusive game drive zones

3. Olare Motorogi Conservancy

  • Why here: One of the most wildlife-dense areas of Kenya
  • Top Lodges: Mahali Mzuri (Richard Branson's Camp), Mara Plains
  • Unique Edge: Luxurious, intimate, conservation-focused

4. Maasai Mara National Reserve (Main Reserve)

  • Why here: Close proximity to the Mara River crossings
  • Top Lodges: Governors' Camp, Entim Mara
  • Unique Edge: Central to migration action, especially July-August

What to Expect During a Migration Safari

  • River Crossings: Watch wildebeest brave crocodile-infested waters at points like the Talek and Mara Rivers.
  • Predator Encounters: Lions, leopards, and cheetahs hunt opportunistically during the chaos.
  • Dramatic Landscapes: Endless golden plains punctuated by acacia trees and stormy skies.
  • Cultural Interactions: Many conservancies offer visits to nearby Maasai villages.

Tips for Planning Your Migration Safari

  1. Book Early: Lodges fill up 6–12 months in advance.
  2. Pack for Variability: Mornings are cold; midday is warm.
  3. Use a Reputable Operator: Sundown Safari curates migration itineraries with expert guides.
  4. Fly-In Safaris: Save time and energy by flying directly into the Mara from Nairobi.
