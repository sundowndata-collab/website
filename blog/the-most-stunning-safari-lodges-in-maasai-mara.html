The Most Stunning Safari Lodges in Maasai Mara

Published: July 22, 2025  ·  Sundown Safaris Field Team

Introduction: Where Luxury Meets the Wild

There’s no safari destination quite like the Maasai Mara. Famed for its sprawling plains, Big Five sightings, and the awe-inspiring Great Migration, this Kenyan reserve also offers some of the most exceptional safari lodges in Africa. Whether you're seeking romance, family-friendly comfort, or eco-conscious luxury, there’s a lodge in Maasai Mara to suit your style.

In this guide, we unveil the most stunning Maasai Mara safari lodges—where every sunrise, sound, and safari drive is wrapped in world-class comfort.

1. Angama Mara: Where Heaven Meets Earth

Perched high above the Great Rift Valley, Angama Mara offers breathtaking views of the Mara Triangle below. The tented suites feature floor-to-ceiling glass, letting nature in without sacrificing luxury.

  • Private decks and personal butlers
  • Exceptional cuisine and curated bush picnics
  • Exclusive access to less crowded game areas

Why Stay Here? It’s the perfect spot for honeymoons, proposals, and photography lovers.

2. Mahali Mzuri: Sir Richard Branson’s Safari Camp

Located in the private Olare Motorogi Conservancy, Mahali Mzuri blends modern design with authentic safari spirit.

  • 12 high-end tents with ensuite bathrooms
  • Excellent year-round wildlife viewing
  • Community-based conservation efforts

Why Stay Here? Ideal for travelers looking for five-star amenities with intimate wildlife encounters.

3. Sand River Masai Mara: Colonial Charm Meets Wilderness

Designed to replicate the 1920s safari era, this camp takes you back in time while offering modern comforts.

  • Elegant tented suites with freestanding baths
  • Located near the Tanzania border for migration crossings
  • Fine dining and guided game drives

Why Stay Here? Perfect for history buffs and romantic getaways.

4. Mara Plains Camp: Exclusive Luxury in the Heart of the Wild

Situated in the Olare Motorogi Conservancy, this Relais & Châteaux camp offers a secluded experience.

  • Only seven luxury tents
  • Swarovski binoculars in every room
  • Personalized safari experiences

Why Stay Here? For travelers who value privacy, sustainability, and ultra-luxury.

5. Kichwa Tembo Tented Camp: Best for Families and First-Timers

This camp, run by &Beyond, sits on a private concession along the Mara River.

  • Classic tents and superior tents with pool access
  • Family-friendly activities and kids’ safaris
  • Exceptional hospitality and wildlife viewings

Why Stay Here? Great value for families, groups, and those new to safaris.

Tips for Choosing the Right Lodge

  • Travel Season: Choose camps near migration paths during peak months (July to October).
  • Interests: Birding, photography, cultural visits? Pick lodges that specialize.
  • Privacy: Some lodges are better for seclusion and romance.
  • Budget: Luxury comes at many levels—from ultra-exclusive to comfortable charm.
